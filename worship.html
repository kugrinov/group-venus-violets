Worship

Altars

Altars are a very common way to worship many deities, especially the Greek gods, goddesses, and even heroes. There are many types of altars that can be crafted including temporary and travel altars for people who may not be able to worship their gods openly. Most altars start with the incense cleansing of a space, candles, statues, and items that remind the worshipper of the god such as flowers, crystals, trinkets, or any personal touches. There are many different types of altars, but the main thing to be aware of is that the space needs to be designated to that deity because anything that is set on the altar, even by accident, will be seen as an offering, and is difficult to get back (especially if the god you worship is stubborn or extremely playful).

Prayer

Prayer is a common way that many people worship gods or goddesses. It is a way to communicate with the god or goddess you are trying to worship directly. Many people use prayer beads as a way to help them feel more connected, and those usually consist of chains and beads along with charms that the user feels represents their god or goddess. Prayers are very personal and can be very simple or vy complex depending on what the devotee feels is right. There is no real wrong way to pray, just remember to be respectful and considerate when praying. You can also use this to ask for signs or symbols that can help let you know your god or goddess is listening.

Offerings

Offerings go hand in hand with altars for the gods and goddesses. Many people will leave a designated spot like a platform or bowl on top of their altars as an offering area. These offerings can include food, drinks, gems, handmade crafts, flowers, and much more. It is also a good idea to light the candle you have on your designated altar and look into flame communication to try and see if your god or goddess is accepting of the offer or would prefer that you remove it. There is no rhyme or reason to offerings, it can be scheduled or at random. But it can be especially helpful when asking for guidance from a specific god because it shows a sort of devotion besides just expecting a reward.
